# font J.J. Naglas

Original decorative, all-caps font with single typeface inspired when tracing serif façade writing in CAD.


All glyphs are inscribed inside a circle (which originally defined the lower edge of the J on the façade) and constructed using mainly circular arcs and fewer straight edges. 
The circles are split radially into eigths and linearly into halves or thirds to produce "control points" at the intersections from which the glyph was then constructed.

Each letter was individually modelled after all its variants in the latin script chosen based on how well those shapes would translate into an inscribed circle: `A C D G J K M N Q U W X Z` are modelled after their capital variants, `B E F H I L O P T V Y` after their lower variants and `R & S` cursive variants.
The font includes diacritics suitable for most European languages, however available glyphs may vary.
